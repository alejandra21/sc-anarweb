These publications are part of the teaching materials that ANAR is developing, to encourage and protect interested in the rock heritage.

One of the goals of this material is to provide support for teachers, students, schools and communities; also they provide information to different sectors of society.

They are available to individuals and institutions. The publications are on sale in the ANAR. If you prefer you can contact us through our email: anarvenezuela@gmail.com and anar@anar.org.ve.. Gladly we will send. Do not hesitate to contact us.


The Design of Venezuelans Petroglyphs
Ruby De Valencia & Jeannine Sujo


It's content permited:

  • Present the first documented photographically over the entire rock stations nationwide recorded so far, which form part of the archaeological heritage of Venezuela inventory.
  • Sustain important findings in the field of anthropological research concerning Venezuela, based on the study and interrelation of the stylistic characteristics of these stations; the stylistic horizons that define the different geographical areas of the country were made. These horizons allowed in turn guide several hypotheses concerning some of the oldest migratory routes of our country.
Trilingual edition of the Pampero Foundation, Caracas, 1987, 410 pages.

This publication was credited with the following national and international awards:

  • Representing Venezuela at Exposición EL LIBRO MAS BELLO DEL MUNDO 1988.
  • Bronce Medal; at Exposición Internacional del Arte del Libro, 1989.
  • Honor Diploma, Leipzig, Alemania.
  • Acknowledgement at IX Exposición Anual del Libro Ilustrado y la Fotografía Documental , 1988, Instituto Autónomo Biblioteca Nacional, Caracas.


Venezuela Rupestre - Mundo Rupestre


Through rotating wheels, this educational game offers a thematic tour of Venezuela and the World Cave Rock, showing its unity, diversity, and how to understand the world through rock art.


Rupestrian UNO: Venezuelan Rupestrian Manifestations


This unique game will allow educators apply various strategies for teaching and learning with the Cultural Heritage Archaeological. Letters containing, allow turn the game into an educational experience them for information on Venezuelan rock art and the states where they are located.

Students, educators and the general public of all ages can enter the world of rock art, through this popular science educational game, which aims to promote knowledge as a vehicle for the protection of rock art sites in Venezuela.
